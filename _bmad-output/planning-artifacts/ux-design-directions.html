D1 — Pure Minimal

Almost no UI chrome. Typography does all the work. Maximum white space. Signals "we trust you" rather than guiding you. Inspired by Signal's restraint. The product identity comes entirely from copy and color accent — not structure.

The baseline "Restrained Civic" direction. Cards, soft shadows, amber accent used warmly. Feels human and approachable without being soft. The primary + amber palette is non-partisan but clearly civic. Closest to what was specified in the Visual Foundation step.

D3 — Editorial

Newspaper/journalism aesthetic. Strong typographic hierarchy, double rules, kickers, footnote-style citations. Signals credibility and accountability. The product feels like it belongs next to journalism, not tech. High trust signal for the AI-skeptical audience — this looks like something that takes facts seriously.

D4 — App-Forward

Progress steps, input-group cards, app-like chrome. Makes the flow feel guided and structured — clearer for first-time users who want to know where they are. More friction by design; more hand-holding. Better for users who feel anxious without visible structure (like Deb). Tradeoff: feels more like software, less like a human tool.

D5 — High Contrast Accessible

Maximum contrast, larger type defaults, minimal decoration. Black and white primary palette with amber highlights only. Designed to work for users with low vision, older screens, and bright sunlight (outdoor/phone context). Every element passes WCAG AAA. Least visually distinctive but highest accessibility floor.
